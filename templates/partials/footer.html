About PADD

PADD - Personal Assistant for Daily Documentation

Or, in Star Trek terms, a "Personal Access Display Device"

PADD is a simple, local, markdown-based personal information system for capturing and organizing your thoughts, tasks, and knowledge. Like the handheld devices in Star Trek, PADD serves as your personal command center - but instead of managing a starship, you're managing your daily workflow and information.

The system uses a simple capture → process → execute → store workflow with three core files and a resources directory:

  • inbox.md: Quick capture point for notes, ideas, and tasks - dump everything here without thinking
  • active.md: Your dashboard of current commitments - what needs attention now
  • daily/: Daily log for activities, thoughts, and reflections - your temporal record
  • journal: Personal reflections, ideas, and insights - your private space
  • resources/: Organized reference material by topic - where processed information lives long-term

Resources Organization Examples: 

resources/
├── someday.md           # Ideas and projects for the future
├── people/              # Notes about colleagues, contacts
│   └── john-smith.md
├── projects/            # Completed or reference project notes
│   ├── website-redesign.md
│   └── 2024-planning.md
├── reference/           # General reference material
│   ├── commands.md      # Useful commands and snippets
│   └── workflows.md     # Process documentation
├── learning/            # Course notes, articles, research
│   └── python-notes.md
└── meetings/            # Meeting notes and decisions
    └── 2024-01-standup.md

Workflow: Everything starts in inbox.md. During regular processing, move items from inbox to either active.md (needs action), resources/ (reference material), or trash. Work from active.md as your command center. When done, move notes to the appropriate resources/ folder. Daily.md captures the journey - what happened and when - as a permanent chronological record.
